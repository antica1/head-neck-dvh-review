# Clinical Pearls from 2026-07-10 Session — Head & Neck DVH Review

## QUANTEC Limitations (Critical Context)

All neuro-OAR constraints originate from animal models (rat/guinea pig spinal cord, monkey brainstem) at full cross-section, 2 Gy/fx, single course. Four dimensions missing:

1. **Partial volume** — clinical IMRT only irradiates anterior cord/brainstem surface (1-4 mm depth), not full cross-section
2. **Fractionation** — NPC ENI at 50 Gy/30fx (1.67 Gy/fx) has BED₃ equivalent of only 46 Gy at 2 Gy/fx
3. **Re-irradiation** — no human data on cumulative tolerance; ~50% repair at 1+ year gap
4. **Age/tradeoff** — young curative patients accept higher OAR risk than elderly palliative

## Compartment Irradiation Principle (间室放疗)

For skull base/orbital/post-operative tumors: irradiate the ENTIRE involved compartment, not just GTV + N mm margin. "Door and next room" metaphor:
- Involved compartment → full coverage
- Low-grade: irradiate the "door" (orbital apex, foramina) at prophylactic dose (50-54 Gy)  
- High-grade: door already breached → irradiate "next room" (cavernous sinus) at radical dose (66 Gy)

## NPC-Specific Boundaries ≠ Non-NPC Boundaries

CRITICAL PITFALL: Lancet/IG NPC guidelines modified Level III anterior border from sternohyoid lateral edge to SCM posterior edge. This is NPC-only. Non-NPC HNC MUST use the original wider boundary or risk missing anterior neck disease (oral cavity, larynx, hypopharynx).

## SBRT + IO Immunogenic Threshold

- Single fraction 3.5 Gy is below optimal ICD window (6-12 Gy)
- Quad-Shot (3.7 Gy × 4 BID) crosses threshold through cumulative antigen release
- Q3w single fraction approach = safety compromise when prior RT dose is high, not maximal immune effect
